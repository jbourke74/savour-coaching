**Idea 1: Slow Food Editorial**

- **Design Movement:** Italian slow-food editorial meets contemporary wellness
- **Core Principles:** Unhurried pacing, sensory richness, warmth over polish, intimacy
- **Color Philosophy:** Warm cream (#FAF6F0) background, deep terracotta (#B5522B) as accent, forest sage (#4A6741) for secondary, near-black charcoal (#1C1A17) for text. Evokes the warmth of a sun-drenched kitchen table.
- **Layout Paradigm:** Asymmetric editorial — large pull-quotes float left while body text runs right; sections alternate between full-bleed imagery and tight text columns
- **Signature Elements:** Handwritten-style serif headings (Playfair Display), generous line-height body text, soft watercolour dividers
- **Interaction Philosophy:** Slow scroll reveals, text fades in gently — the page breathes
- **Animation:** Subtle fade-up on scroll, no jarring transitions; everything feels like turning a page
- **Typography System:** Playfair Display (headings, pull-quotes) + Lora (body) — warm, literary, feminine without being fussy

**Idea 2: Organic Modernism**

- **Design Movement:** Organic modernism — the intersection of Bauhaus structure and natural imperfection
- **Core Principles:** Structured yet soft, honest materials, purposeful asymmetry, breathing room
- **Color Philosophy:** Off-white linen (#F5F0E8) base, deep olive (#3D4A2E) as primary, warm gold (#C9A84C) as accent, dusty rose (#D4A5A5) as a soft highlight. Feels like a beautifully designed cookbook.
- **Layout Paradigm:** Offset grid — content blocks sit at deliberate angles to the baseline; hero text is left-aligned and large; testimonials break the grid intentionally
- **Signature Elements:** Thin horizontal rules, large numbered section markers, organic blob shapes as background accents
- **Interaction Philosophy:** Hover states reveal warmth — buttons bloom with a soft glow; links underline with a slow draw
- **Animation:** Staggered entrance animations on section load; parallax on hero image
- **Typography System:** Cormorant Garamond (display, large headings) + DM Sans (body, navigation) — elegant contrast between old-world serif and clean modern sans

**Idea 3: Intimate Journal**

- **Design Movement:** Personal journal meets luxury wellness brand
- **Core Principles:** Intimacy, vulnerability, handcrafted feel, warmth
- **Color Philosophy:** Warm ivory (#FDFAF5) background, deep plum (#5C3D4E) as primary, terracotta (#C47B5A) as accent, sage (#7A9E7E) as a calming secondary. Feels like a well-loved notebook.
- **Layout Paradigm:** Single-column narrative flow with generous margins; occasional full-bleed imagery; pull-quotes break the column width dramatically
- **Signature Elements:** Decorative initial caps on section openers, subtle paper texture on background, hand-drawn botanical line art as dividers
- **Interaction Philosophy:** The page feels like reading a letter; CTAs feel like an invitation, not a demand
- **Animation:** Text reveals line by line on scroll; images bloom into view with a soft scale
- **Typography System:** Libre Baskerville (headings, pull-quotes) + Source Serif 4 (body) — both serif, creating a unified literary feel with clear hierarchy
</text>
